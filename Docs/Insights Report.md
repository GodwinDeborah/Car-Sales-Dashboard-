# Car Sales Dashboard | Insights Report

## Overview

This dashboard explores vehicle sales performance across manufacturers, dealer regions, body styles, and customer preferences. The analysis highlights trends in sales activity, pricing patterns, vehicle categories, and regional performance.

---

# Key Insights

## 1. Sales Performance Shows Strong Growth Trends

The dashboard reports $371.19M in YTD total sales, representing a 23.59% year-over-year increase, alongside 13.26K cars sold (+24.75% YOY). Growth in both revenue and units suggests that increased sales volume, rather than pricing changes, is the primary driver of overall performance.

---

## 2. Average Selling Price Remains Relatively Stable

The YTD average selling price is $27.99K, with a slight YOY decline of 0.79%. Despite strong growth in sales volume, pricing remains largely unchanged, indicating that revenue growth is driven more by demand than price adjustments.

---

## 3. Weekly Sales Activity Shows Periodic Fluctuations

Sales activity varies across the year, with noticeable peaks in several later weeks. These fluctuations suggest uneven demand distribution over time, potentially reflecting seasonal effects or changes in customer purchasing behavior.

---

## 4. SUVs Dominate Sales by Body Style

SUVs lead all body styles with $99.89M in YTD sales, followed by Hatchbacks ($82.77M), Sedans ($73.69M), Passenger vehicles ($63.44M), and Hardtops ($51.40M). This distribution highlights a clear customer preference for SUVs, while Hardtops represent the lowest-performing segment.

---

## 5. Pale White and Black Are the Most Popular Vehicle Colors

Pale White vehicles account for the highest sales at $174.53M, followed by Black at $125.24M and Red at $71.41M. The dominance of neutral colors suggests a strong customer preference for standard, resale-friendly vehicle options.

---

## 6. Sales Performance Varies Across Dealer Regions

Dealer region performance differs significantly, with Austin, Janesville, and Scottsdale recording higher sales compared to other locations. This indicates uneven regional demand and highlights certain geographic areas as stronger sales markets.

---

## 7. Automatic Vehicles Outperform Manual Across All Regions

Automatic transmission vehicles consistently generate higher sales than manual vehicles across all dealer regions. This reflects a clear market preference for automatic vehicles within the dataset.

---

## 8. Manufacturer Contributions Vary Across Brands

Sales performance is uneven across manufacturers, with Chevrolet, Ford, and Dodge among the leading contributors. This indicates differences in brand strength and customer preference across the automotive market.

---

# Conclusion

The dashboard reveals strong year-over-year growth in both sales revenue and units sold, supported by stable pricing and increasing demand. SUVs and automatic vehicles dominate customer preferences, while color choices remain concentrated in neutral options like white and black. Regional and manufacturer analysis shows clear variations in performance, highlighting key growth areas and stronger market segments. These insights provide a foundation for understanding sales trends, customer behavior, and business performance across the dataset.
